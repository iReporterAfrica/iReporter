How to Write a Good iReport

Correct spelling, grammar and punctuation are critical. Use grammar, syntax and writing with a level of dedication that shows the situation is critical and need urgent response. Never use text-style abbreviations or rude or profane language.

The report is your megaphone — your view of the situation and your passion will determine the success of your red flag or intervention it is not just regurgitation of memorized scenarios. It needs to tell a story with passion, using personal experiences, action anecdotes that showcase your interests, your values for life, your views of the scenario and even your goal.
